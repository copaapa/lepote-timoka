Monastery of the Holy Apostles Peter and Paul in Grliste

The Monastery of the Holy Apostles Peter and Paul in Grliste, or Grlište Monastery, is located in the village of the same name, 14 km away from Zaječar. Within the monastery there are old icons made on wood, which are considered to have a miraculous effect.

This monastery falls under the Diocese of Timok of the Serbian Orthodox Church. Based on written data, it is believed that it originated in the 12th century, but it is not known who was the founder of the monastery. It was built in the Gothic style.

The height of the monastery is 4.5 meters without the dome, which is 2.5 meters high. It is 4.5 meters wide and 13.5 meters long. It consists of a narthex, porch and utility room. The bell tower consists of two bells, and dates from 1885.

Until the Serbian-Turkish war of 1876, there was an old wooden iconostasis within the monastery, but due to burning monastery by the Turks, only 8 icons remain. These icons are considered to be the oldest icons from Eastern Serbia.
