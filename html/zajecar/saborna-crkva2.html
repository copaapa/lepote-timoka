The Orthodox Church of the Nativity of the Holy Virgin in Zajecar

Until the 1830s, Zajecar did not have its own church because of the Turks, and later because of the Bulgarians. Only after Serbia became an autonomy under the Turkish protectorate in 1833, a year later, the construction of a small chapel in Zajecar began, and later a church. The church was completed in October 1834 and is dedicated to the Birth of the Most Holy Mother of God.

Like most other churches in the Timok region, this church was not spared from damage by the Turks. in the Serbian-Turkish war of 1876-1878. Icons were destroyed and replaced with new ones in 1880. 1898 the narthex was added to the church, and in 1899 the church received five bells. New bells were placed at the beginning of the 21st century.

There is one interesting fact related to this church and the diocese of Timok. The seat of the mentioned diocese was in the church in Zajecar, but due to the small number of responses of believers at the Sunday liturgy, and the dissatisfaction of the then bishop, the seat of the diocese was moved to Negotin until 1890, where the seat of the diocese is moved again to Zajecar.
