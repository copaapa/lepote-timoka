Brestovacka spa

Brestovacka Banja is one of the oldest spas in Serbia. It was created during the reign of Prince Miloš Obrenović in the 1930s. In 1837, a Turkish hammam and the residence of Prince Miloš were built, which are today under state protection. Also, in 1856, the castle of Prince Aleksandar Krađorđević was built, and in 1906, the summer house of King Peter I Karađorđević was built.

This spa is located at an altitude of 385 meters on the banks of the Brestovacka river and the Pujica river, surrounded by a century-old forest, and the town of Bor is 8 kilometers away from the spa. According to archaeological sources, it is believed that the springs of this spa were also used by the ancient Romans.

The Austrian Baron Herder compared the waters of this spa with the springs of the Swiss and Austrian spas. On the healing properties of water from this spa affects the content of chemical elements and trace elements, such as calcium, potassium, sodium, fluorine, chlorine, silicon, iodine, bromine, magnesium, zinc and many others. There are a number of thermo-mineral springs within this spa whose temperature goes up to 40 degrees Celsius.

In this spa, patients who have problems with the spine, bones, joints and skin muscles are treated. Also, the waters from Brestovacka spa help in the treatment of general exhaustion of the organism, the consequence of rheumatic injuries, peripheral blood vessel disease and inflammation of the female genitals.

The following accommodation capacities work in the spa: "Srpska kruna", "ZIJIN Klub", and villas "Lucija", "Toplica", "Košuta", "Biljana".
