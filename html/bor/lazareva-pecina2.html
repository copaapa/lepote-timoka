Lazar's cave

Lazar's Cave, popularly known as Zlotska Cave, is 3 kilometers away from the village of Zlot and 21 kilometers from the town of Bor. It is one of the longest caves in Serbia. It is located at an altitude of 291 meters, and is positioned on the left side of the Lazarev River. This cave was opened to tourists in the 1950s, and the tourist trail is 900 meters long.

The first research on Lazarus Cave was conducted by Felix Hoffman in the 1980s, and a few years after him, speleological research was carried out by Jovan CvijiÄ‡. It is estimated that the volume of the cave is over 52,000 cubic meters, and 16,000 meters of space have been explored.

This cave also has a very important archeological significance. Ceramic objects were discovered in it, bone and copper tools from the Copper Age, and it is believed that during the Bronze Age Lazar's cave had the role of a hunting station, and in time Iron Age was the center of metallurgy. Remains of an extinct cave bear, a cave lion and a cave hyena were also found in it.

The cave space intended for tourists consists of beautiful halls: Preston Hall, Block Hall, Concert Hall, Bat Hall. Today's inhabitants of Lazar's Cave are bats.

The working hours of Lazar's Cave are from Wednesday to Sunday from 10 am to 4 pm, but it is also possible to work on Tuesdays for organized groups with at least 20 people. It won't work on Mondays. Ticket prices are 200 dinars, and for organized groups and students 150 dinars.
