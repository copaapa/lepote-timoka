Zoo Bor

Bor ZOO was opened in November 2011, and covers an area of ​​2 hectares, where there are over 70 species of animals. It is located in Bor itself. The ambience of this zoo is adapted to the natural habitat of animals. There used to be a forest in the area where the ZOO was built, so the builders tried to preserve it as much as possible. original appearance. Within the garden there is a cafe, where visitors can relax and refresh in natural environment surrounded by deciduous forest.

As part of the ZOO, various events are held every year, such as Wildlife Days, Dedamrazijada, various celebrations can also be organized in the zoo, such as birthdays for the youngest.

All animals have fully equipped habitats so that they do not even feel winter. Animals coming from the African continent are located in adequately walled rooms that heat them, where they even have underfloor heating.

The biggest attraction of the Bor Zoo is the white lion Dušan, and other species of animals are represented, such as: coyotes, wolves, tiger, foxes, antelope, zebra, Tibetan bear, Berber sheep, two-humped camels, yaks, kangaroos, mercats, monkeys, crocodiles, ostriches, various snakes and other animals.

The ticket price is 100 dinars, and children up to 12 years of age do not pay the entrance fee. The working hours of the garden vary from month to month, so during the summer it works from 10 to 20 hours, and during the fall and winter from 10 a.m. to 3 p.m. or from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Bor Zoo is located in Dr. Milovanovića bb.
