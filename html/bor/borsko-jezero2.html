Borsko lake

Lake Bor is an artificial lake that was created in 1959 by building a dam and accumulating water from the following surrounding rivers in the municipality of Bor: Zlotske reke, Valja Žoni and Marecove reke. The lake is located at the foot of the mountain Crni vrh. The lake is 15 kilometers away from the town of Bor. It is located at an altitude of 438 meters.

The water from this lake is primarily intended for the needs of the company ZIJIN, the former Mining and Smelting Basin Bor, but it is also used for rest and recreation of Boran and citizens from the surrounding towns. Many citizens of Bor built cottages along the coast lakes. Lake Bor occupies an area of ​​30 hectares, and the greatest depth is 52 meters. This lake is one of the few artificial lakes in Serbia that has a very indented shore and an undefined shape.

The water in this lake is clear, and even in some parts it is safe to drink. Apart from having a tourist character, this lake is a paradise for fishermen. During the summer, the water temperature reaches 25 degrees Celsius, where, thanks to that, it attracts a large number of guests from all over Serbia.

Many tourists who visit Bor and Bor Lake consider this to be a lake unique thanks to the beautiful nature that surrounds it, such as deciduous and coniferous forests. Within the lake there are also paths for walking and enjoying.

Several guest accommodation facilities have been built on the shores of the lake, such as the Jezero Hotel. Villa "Vertigo" and apartment "Beach", and there is also a car camp Borsko Lake in which there are about 300 caravans.
