Bogovinska pećina

In the area of ​​the village of Bogovina, in the Boljevac municipality, there is the Bogovinska cave. It is 12 kilometers away from Boljevac. About six kilometers of the cave have been explored so far and it is one of the longest in Serbia.

This cave was first explored in the 1930s, and it is surprising that our famous speleologist and researcher, Jovan Cvijić, did not know about it, and he passed through this area. It is believed that the locals deliberately hid this cave from him in order to continue to use it as shelters, escapes and the like.

Bogovinska cave is mostly built in Mesozoic limestones of the Middle and Upper Jurassic and Lower Cretaceous. The maximum thickness of limestone in the cave zone is 170 meters. Inside the cave, there is a river that flows through it, where the basin of the Bogovinska river was built. Fossil remains of an extinct Pleistocene rhinoceros were discovered in the cave.

In the part of the cave that is intended for tourists, there are several halls. Concert hall lined with monumental decorations. The Mining Hall is located on the terrace on the right side of the river channel and is dedicated to the goddess miners. The Cyclops Hall has an abundance of jewelry that are predominantly stalagmite groups. The Hajduk hall is named after the yellow stalagmite, which is ten meters high meters and symbolizes the hero of the First Serbian Uprising, Hajduk Veljko Petrovic.
