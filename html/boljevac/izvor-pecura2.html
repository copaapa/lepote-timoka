Spring Pecura

The spring of Pecur is actually the source of the river Crni Timok, which is located at the foot of the Kucaj mountains in the village of Krivi Vir in the Boljevac municipality. It is composed of two sources, but they are not interconnected. Chief the spring is located at the beginning of the Pecur gorge, from where water springs under massive rocks, and in part from a large cave. This spring has a continuous source of water, with minor interruptions during dry periods, but it has never been recorded that it has ever dried up.

The second spring is somewhat weaker and is located in a small cave, near the remains of a former old mill, and he, unfortunately, it often dries up in summer. There was a small spring near that spring before the Second World War a hydroelectric power plant that supplied the entire village with electricity.

Downstream of these springs there is a complex of several springs of thermo-mineral water, whose temperature is about 22 degrees Celsius.
