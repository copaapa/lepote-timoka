Lapusnja monastery

The Lapusnja Monastery is 2 kilometers away from the main road Paracin - Zajecar, near the village of Lukovo near Boljevac. This monastery can be reached by an unpaved road in the direction of Rtnje. The exact location of this monastery is at the foot of the hill Vučja Glava is also located in the bay next to Lopušanski potok.

This monastery is dedicated to the Holy Father Nicholas the Wonderworker, and thanks to the Turkish census in 1455. it is known that it was built before the Turkish conquests. The reconstruction of the monastery was financed by Duke Jovan Radul and the prefect Gergina in 1500, and the monastery church was painted in 1510.

The church in Lapusnja has a triconchonal base, with a narthex on the west and a semicircular altar apse on the east. The altar part is divided into three parts, as well as the nave which consists of three aisles, but the dimensions are not the same. An eight-sided dome also rises, resting on a square base. Unfortunately, the fresco painting of the church is very poorly preserved and has faded, since it was demolished several times. Today, this monastery belongs to the cultural monuments of great importance in Serbia.
