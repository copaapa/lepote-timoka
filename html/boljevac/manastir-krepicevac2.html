Krepicevac monastery

Krepicevac Monastery is located in the Boljevac municipality, and is located 4 kilometers north of the village of Jablanica, in the gorge of the Radovanska river. This monastery belongs to the Diocese of Timok of the Serbian Orthodox Church and is dedicated to the Assumption of the Most Holy Mother of God.

The founder of this monastery is considered to be the prefect of Georgia, who holds a model of the church on the founder's composition. The time of construction of this monastery is connected to the end of the 15th century and the beginning of the 16th century. There are very few preserved records regarding this monastery. It is mentioned as an active monastery in the 18th century. At the beginning of the 19th century, during the First Serbian Uprising, the hajduk Veljko appointed Hieromonk Akaki from Mount Athos as the head of this monastery in 1809. Before the outbreak of World War II, the monastery became a nunnery.

One of the latest medieval donor compositions in Serbia has been preserved, where they are located on the north wall of the narthex of the Krepiƒçevac monastery. In the lower part of the monastery, figures of saints have been preserved, Archangel Michael, St. Pachomius and St. Constantine and Helen. In the altar of the monastery is a composition of the Vision of St. Peter of Alexandria. The narthex also contains compositions from the life of Christ and the Mother of God, where the Escape to Egypt stands out the most.

Today, unfortunately, there are no inhabitants of this monastery, and it is served by parish priests.
